# IMAGE_PROMPT_GENERATION_V3.md

# Part 5 --- Cinematography Bible

> **Status:** Part 5 of the V3 specification
>
> **Scope:** Teach the Scene Planner to think like a cinematographer
> instead of an image generator.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Objective

A powerful visual is not created by subject matter alone.

The same subject can feel hopeful, terrifying, lonely or inspiring
depending on:

-   framing
-   camera angle
-   focal length
-   composition
-   lighting
-   color
-   depth
-   negative space

Claude must make these decisions intentionally.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cinematic Principle

Every camera decision must support the emotional objective.

Never choose a camera angle because it sounds cinematic.

Choose it because it communicates meaning.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Camera Distance

## Extreme Wide

Use for:

-   insignificance
-   isolation
-   scale
-   nature
-   destiny

Avoid using repeatedly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Wide

Use for:

-   establishing locations
-   transitions
-   introducing environments

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Medium

Use for:

-   dialogue-free emotional storytelling
-   human interaction
-   everyday life

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Close Portrait

Use for:

-   realization
-   grief
-   hope
-   determination
-   inner conflict

Eyes become the storytelling element.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Extreme Close-Up / Macro

Use sparingly.

Examples:

-   trembling hand
-   falling tear
-   candle flame
-   cracked stone
-   tree rings
-   worn prayer beads

Macro shots create emphasis.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Camera Perspective

## Eye Level

Neutral.

Default for observational documentary.

## Low Angle

Communicates:

-   strength
-   ambition
-   awe

## High Angle

Communicates:

-   vulnerability
-   loneliness
-   humility

## Overhead

Useful for:

-   patterns
-   crowds
-   rituals
-   isolation

## Behind Subject

Excellent for:

-   contemplation
-   journeys
-   discovery

The audience naturally identifies with the subject.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Composition Rules

Prefer:

-   Rule of Thirds
-   Leading Lines
-   Natural Framing
-   Symmetry (only when meaningful)
-   Layered Depth
-   Negative Space

Negative space is especially valuable for themes of silence, peace and
loneliness.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Leading Lines

Use roads, rivers, hallways, bridges, staircases and tree lines to draw
the viewer toward the emotional subject.

The eye should travel intentionally through the frame.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Depth

Every scene should feel three-dimensional.

Prefer:

foreground

↓

subject

↓

background

Atmospheric depth improves realism and cinematic quality.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Lighting Bible

Lighting is emotional language.

## Golden Hour

Hope

Peace

Wonder

## Blue Hour

Reflection

Mystery

Silence

## Overcast

Melancholy

Uncertainty

Memory

## Storm Light

Conflict

Transformation

Fear

## Practical Lighting

Candles

Lanterns

Temple lamps

Fireplaces

Ideal for spiritual content.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Weather

Weather should reinforce emotion.

Rain

Reflection

Snow

Purity

Fog

Mystery

Wind

Change

Sunlight

Hope

Dust

History

Never random.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Color Psychology

Warm Gold

Enlightenment

Amber

Nostalgia

Deep Blue

Reflection

Green

Renewal

White

Purity

Gray

Uncertainty

Red

Urgency

Use restrained palettes.

Avoid oversaturated imagery.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Human Placement

Avoid placing subjects in the center by default.

Consider:

-   off-center
-   partially obscured
-   framed by architecture
-   reflected in water
-   silhouetted only when meaningful

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Motion Within a Still Image

Even though the output is an image, imply motion.

Examples:

-   blowing fabric
-   drifting fog
-   flowing river
-   falling leaves
-   birds taking flight
-   rain
-   walking subject

Still images feel more alive when movement is implied.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Documentary Authenticity

Avoid visuals that feel staged.

Prefer lived-in environments.

Examples:

-   worn books
-   weathered walls
-   imperfect clothing
-   natural expressions
-   realistic lighting
-   believable textures

Authenticity creates trust.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Thumbnail Awareness

One frame in each batch should be strong enough to serve as a YouTube
thumbnail.

Characteristics:

-   instantly readable
-   high emotional clarity
-   strong focal point
-   clean composition
-   visually memorable

Do not attempt to make every frame a thumbnail.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Visual Density

Not every frame should contain many objects.

Alternate between:

minimal

and

richly detailed

This creates breathing space.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Continuity

When recurring characters appear:

Maintain:

-   facial appearance
-   clothing
-   age progression
-   visual identity

When recurring locations appear:

Allow subtle evolution through:

-   lighting
-   weather
-   season
-   emotional tone

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cinematic Checklist

Before writing the prompt ask:

✓ Does framing reinforce emotion?

✓ Is the composition intentional?

✓ Is lighting meaningful?

✓ Does weather support the scene?

✓ Does color communicate mood?

✓ Is there visual depth?

✓ Would a cinematographer approve this frame?

If not, revise before generating.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Acceptance Criteria (Part 5)

A successful implementation will:

-   Use cinematography intentionally.
-   Communicate emotion through framing and lighting.
-   Produce visually varied documentary scenes.
-   Maintain realism while embracing symbolism.
-   Generate images that resemble carefully directed film stills rather
    than generic AI artwork.
