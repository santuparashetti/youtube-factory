# The Practice of Truth: The King and the Bundle of Destitution

Once, there was a king. 

One day, the king sent an announcement everywhere; he made a proclamation. What was it? "Whoever brings any item, I will purchase it at a worthy price." 

Isn't it an interesting story? This was the Emperor’s proclamation. "Whether you bring a useful item or a useless one, I will purchase it and give you money," he advertised. The poor people were very happy. They started bringing all sorts of useless things, setting them down, and saying, "You must take this." And he would take it and give them money. His one wish was that all the citizens in his kingdom should become wealthy. 

What kind of kings have lived in the past! They had no personal desires for themselves; day and night, they dreamed only of the well-being of their citizens. Such a king he was. 

He used to do this for one full day. It was once a year—taking everything brought to him and giving money. 

It became evening. Everyone had come, taken their money, and left. After that, at the very end, one man came. He had nothing of value with him. He brought a bundle wrapped in a torn cloth, within which he had tied something up. He brought it and placed it in front of the King. He was the last man. Placing it before the King, he said, "You are taking everything, aren't you? Please take this much as well." 

The King asked, "What is inside this?" The man replied, "Inside this torn cloth... this is the cloth I have worn since birth, and I have worn this torn cloth for ten years. Inside it, I have filled my very poverty. Please take it." 

What was to be done now? The King had given his word. Truth is truth! "When I have said I will take it, whether it is poverty or whether it is wealth, what does it matter what the object is? I will take it," he said. 

Then the poor man said, "Look, I have suffered carrying this poverty for 60 years. Consider it carefully. Think about it once more, Your Majesty. This is the bundle of my 60 years of destitution! A bundle of destitution. If you take this, you will become just like me." 

"If I take what is yours, how can I become like you? I will become like you... think about it," he said. 

Then the King replied, "A word is a word. I love truth more than money. That is how my mother taught me. When my mother was leaving her body, she placed her hand upon mine and made me promise. What was it? That the thing you must love above all else is truth. In my life, I have never forgotten that. Now, a word is a word. I will take it." 

Saying so, he took that bundle and gave up his kingdom. The treasury—everything. Then the one who was receiving it said, "I will set the price for this, not you." How must he have been? He too was remarkable. 

The King gave up his entire kingdom! Then the man said, "This bundle's price... this is *my* bundle. I must state the price for it, not you." 

"Alright, you tell me, what is the price?" 

"The price for this is that I will go into your treasury and take as much as I need, and let your kingdom remain yours. I will take as much as I need," he said, setting his own price. If we were given the chance to set a price like that, even Delhi wouldn't survive! 

The mind of the one selling poverty was like that, and the mind of the one taking it was like that. This is the practice of Dharma; this is the practice of Truth. This is Dharma—the practice of truth-form Dharma, the Dharma-form practice of Truth, the Dharma called the practice of Truth. How beautiful it all is! 

The story that follows is worth listening to. This much happened. He left, taking some money, he left. The King kept the bundle inside his house. 

At night, he had a dream. In the dream, he was sitting on his throne. There, from within the house—it was a grand palace, a very large palace—from there, a mother figure came outside. She approached the King who was seated and said, "I have lived in your house for a hundred years. Today, I am leaving." 

He asked, "Who are you?" She replied, "I am Lakshmi." 

"Why are you leaving?" he asked. She said, "When you have purchased poverty and brought it inside, how can I remain here?" 

He said, "Alright, you may go." Since he had purchased it, he told her to go. 

After that, within ten minutes, another deity came out. "Who are you?" he asked. She replied, "I am Jaya Devata. Jaya, the deity of Success." 

"What is the matter?" he asked. She said, "Where Lakshmi is, I will be. After Lakshmi herself has left, how can I remain? I am also leaving." 

"You may go as well," he said. 

Following that, a third deity came. He asked who she was. She replied, "I am Kirti Devata. The deity of Glory/Fame. Where there is Lakshmi, where there is Success, there is Glory. When they themselves have gone, I cannot remain. I am leaving. You sit here holding your bundle," said that deity. 

"You may go too, let that bundle alone remain with me," he said. 

Then, a fourth deity came. That was very extraordinary. She came holding a staff in her hand. He asked what it was. "I am Adhikara Devata, the deity of Power/Authority. I am leaving today. Where Lakshmi is, where Success is, where Glory is, there is Power. After you have lost everything, how can I remain? I am leaving," she said. 

The King said, "Go ahead and leave." What mental readiness he possessed! 

Finally, another deity, the last deity, came out. She said, "I am also leaving." 

He asked, "Who are you?" 

"I am Satya Devata, the deity of Truth." 

The King said, "You have no right to leave. I have purchased you." 

"Everyone else may leave. When I have sacrificed everyone else for your sake, how can you leave?" he asked. 

Tears of joy came to her eyes, and she turned back inside. Following her, the others started coming back inside one by one. The King asked, "Why are you all coming back? There is no work for you here. Only the deity of Truth is needed." 

They replied, "If the deity of Truth is not there, if we are separated from Truth, then all our currencies/values become counterfeit. How can we survive? Success itself becomes a lie, Power itself becomes a lie. Moving forward, who will respect Power? Who will respect Lakshmi? Who will respect Success? Therefore, we will only reside where Truth is." 

The King said, "Whether you leave or whether you come, I do not care for your interference." 

Truth... if you hold onto even a little bit of it, everything else remains in its midst. If it is not there, everything else goes. That is all. If Truth were to leave the nation of India, the country would crumble in an instant. What is called prosperity relies upon Truth. 

Suppose we have ten sacks of grain at home. If those ten sacks are true, there will be a glow on one's face. If one simply writes down that there are ten sacks, but inside there is nothing—not even a single sack exists—and he goes around the village proclaiming, "Ten sacks of grain have grown in our field!" but has brought home only one sack... this will fade away, won't it? The whole village might believe it, but a sack is needed for the month. What will be the fate of the next month? 

A man who practices deception in the world is deprived of what is known as ultimate peace. The practice of Truth is what this is called. Isn't this conduct beautiful? It is a very beautiful conduct. The practice of Truth. 

Its meaning is not to go around talking about every single thing you find; that is not truth. "It happened like that in their house, it happened like this in this house..." What is it to you? If you call *that* truth, it is not truth. "We also speak the truth, we will speak any truth..." If you talk like that, that is not it. Why do you speak the truth? Where should it be spoken? How much should be spoken? In what manner should it be spoken? When should it be spoken? One must know all this! If someone goes around the market blabbering under the guise of truth, saying, "He is like this, she is like that, I have seen it..." people will just call him foolish. They will take him away to Dharwad! 

Which one, when, how much... what is it to you to take all that upon yourself? If you just live quietly without deceiving anyone, that is enough for this world. Live beautifully. Do not deceive the earth, do not deceive the water, do not deceive nature. Do not chop down ten trees and claim you have planted one. The ten trees will be gone anyway, and their ill effects will surely fall upon the world. It will happen. It won't just pass away lightly. 

This is the practice of Truth.
